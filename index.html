/ Coming up: WALPURGIS NIGHT

Halloween, the pinnacle event for horror aficionados, often overshadows its lesser-known sibling: Walpurgis Night. Rooted in European tradition, it emerges as a captivating counterpart to the October festivities. As the veil between worlds thins, Walpurgis Night signals the arrival of spring with a macabre twist, beckoning those with a penchant for the eerie and the mysterious. Explore this enigmatic holiday, where ancient rituals and modern fascination intertwine under the moon's ghostly glow.

The German Night of the Witches

Walpurgis Night is a traditional spring festival celebrated on April 30th. Taking place exactly six months before Halloween is not a coincidence as both have their origins in pagan celebrations that marked the changing of the seasons. It holds roots in Germanic folklore and pagan customs, often associated with bonfires, dancing, and warding off evil spirits. The night is steeped in mystique, symbolizing a transition from winter's darkness to the vibrant renewal of spring, where communities gather to revel in the mystical energy of the changing seasons.

Spring has come, the days are getting longer and life shows its sunny side again. To honor this new chapter, the Mystic Creatures follow in old footsteps and want to celebrate the legendary Walpurgis Night with you. Walpurgis Night at Neue Zukunft Open-Air Cinema

The festival with free admission offers a colorful program on all stages of the area. Several films and short films as well as performance and music presentations are on the agenda. For young visitors, a family festival with a children's film program is planned in the afternoon. From 6 PM, open-air concerts will take place on the various stages.

Where: Neue Zukunft at Alt-Stralau

Start: 15.00

Cost: Free

Walpurgisnacht at Holzmarkt 25

Here you will find a colorful family festival right on the Spree. Of course, there will be plenty of music - Walpurgis Night is for dancing, after all. There will also be culinary specialties on the program and lots of fun for children and adults. It means that there has been a lot of hammering, sanding, screwing and airing out. Now the colorful crowd of guests can look forward to new ideas. You will see exactly what is meant by this on site. The open-air season here can only be a good one.

Start: 14.00

Cost: €5

Berlin Alphorn and Yodeling / Walpurgis Night

It's the night when, according to old legends, witches and other mystical beings are said to be particularly active. A popular tradition on this day is the gathering of alphorns, bagpipes and yodelers at Fort Hahneberg in the Staaken, district of Spandau. This impressive event is taking place for the 15th time.

Where: Crossroads Weinmeisterhornweg / Reimerweg / Hahnebergweg

Start: 18.30

Cost: Free / Donations

Black Widow

  • Ingredients:
  • 24 cl vodka
  • 32 cl crème de cassis
  • 12 cl coffee liqueur
  • 12 cl ginger beer
  • 2 tsp activated charcoal
  • blackberries

Combine all ingredients, except ginger beer and blackberries, in a shaker, shake well. Add ice cubes, ginger beer, and garnish with blackberries.
